FAQ

Common questions

Do you submit my music directly to major record labels?

No, and you shouldn't either. Major labels (Sony, Universal, Warner) explicitly enforce strict, legally mandated "no unsolicited material" policies to avoid copyright lawsuits. They only accept submissions through music attorneys or established managers. LabelScoutAI helps you match and pitch to highly active, independent boutique labels where direct, high-quality submissions have a realistic signing rate.

How does the AI matching algorithm work?

When you submit your reference artists, our engine queries the Spotify API to pull their recent discography and inspects the copyright metadata (the "P-line") to find the exact labels behind those releases. We then run an automated web-scraper (using Perplexity) to search each label's web footprint to verify if they are active, check their current demo submission channels, and pull the name of their lead A&R person.

What if a label's submission email or link is outdated?

A&R turnover is high. That is why LabelScoutAI does not rely on a static, outdated database. Our system executes live, programmatic web searches using AI-agent nodes to find the most current guidelines posted on their website or social media Linktrees before generating your report.

Is my unreleased music safe and secure?

Absolutely. LabelScoutAI never downloads, stores, or publicly shares your audio files. We only analyze the text-based link and metadata you provide to evaluate the URL's formatting correctness, keeping your unreleased intellectual property completely private.
